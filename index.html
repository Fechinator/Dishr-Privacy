4. Data Stored Locally Only

The following data is stored exclusively on your device and is never transmitted to any server:

  • Dietary preferences (e.g., Vegan, Keto, Halal)
  • Allergy information (e.g., Peanuts, Gluten)
  • Excluded ingredients
  • Saved recipes and images
  • Onboarding completion status

This data is stored using iOS UserDefaults and remains under your full control.

5. AI Processing

Dishr uses Google's Gemini AI (via Firebase AI Logic) for two purposes:

  • Ingredient Recognition: Your ingredient photos are sent to Google Gemini for analysis. Photos are processed to generate results and are not retained by us.
  • Recipe Generation: Text-based prompts (including your ingredient list and dietary preferences) are sent to generate recipe suggestions. These prompts are processed and not stored.

AI processing is subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

7. Legal Basis (Art. 6 GDPR)

We process your data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Contract Performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): Processing ingredient photos and generating recipes is necessary to provide the core service.
  • Legitimate Interest (Art. 6(1)(f)): Analytics and crash reporting help us maintain and improve app quality.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Where required, we obtain your consent before processing (e.g., camera access).

8. Data Retention

Usage tracking data is retained in Firestore for as long as you use the app. Weekly counters (scans, generations) reset automatically every Monday. Analytics and crash data are retained according to Firebase's standard retention policies (typically 14 months for Analytics).

Locally stored data (recipes, preferences) persists until you delete the app or clear its data.

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate measures to protect your data:

  • Firebase App Check (with DeviceCheck) ensures only the legitimate Dishr app can access our backend services.
  • All network communication uses encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS).
  • Local data is stored using iOS secure storage mechanisms.
  • Firestore security rules restrict data access to the authenticated user.
